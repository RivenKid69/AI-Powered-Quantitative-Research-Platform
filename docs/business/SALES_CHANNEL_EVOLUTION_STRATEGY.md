# Sales Channel Evolution Strategy

## QuantBot AI - From Founder-Led to Multi-Channel GTM

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Date:** December 2025
**Classification:** Internal Strategy Document

---

## Executive Summary

This document addresses the critical weakness of **over-reliance on founder-led sales** by establishing a structured evolution toward multi-channel go-to-market (GTM). Our strategy is built on:

- **Proven SaaS scaling frameworks** (Simon-Kucher, Bessemer)
- **Partnership Pyramid model** (PartnerStack)
- **Industry-specific channel strategies** (Prime broker, data vendor partnerships)
- **Phased transition** preserving founder value while building scalable revenue

**Key Reference:** Per [Simon-Kucher research](https://www.simon-kucher.com/en/insights/scaling-sales-through-startup-growth-framework-evolving-your-revenue-organization):

> "Moving from founder-led sales to a scalable sales organisation is a notorious scale-up challenge. Early GTM success can create bottlenecks as demand outpaces the startup's ad-hoc processes."

## 1. Current State Assessment

### 1.1 Founder-Led Sales Characteristics

| Aspect | Current State | Risk Level |
|--------|--------------|------------|
| **Primary Seller** | Founder/CEO | Critical |
| **Sales Process** | Ad-hoc, relationship-driven | High |
| **Documentation** | Minimal playbooks | High |
| **Channel Mix** | 100% direct | Critical |
| **Pipeline** | Network-dependent | High |

### 1.2 Strengths to Preserve

Per [Kixie](https://www.kixie.com/sales-blog/how-a-founder-led-sales-strategy-can-work-wonders-for-your-b2b-startup/):

> "Founders need to lead that strategy, do the work, and build the processes before recruiting a sales leader."

**Founder Advantages:**
- Deep product knowledge
- Credibility with technical buyers
- Ability to make on-the-spot decisions
- Customer discovery insights feed product roadmap
- High close rate on qualified opportunities

**What to Document:**
- Successful pitch decks and narratives
- Common objections and responses
- Qualification criteria that led to wins
- Pricing discussions and outcomes
- Technical deep-dive patterns

### 1.3 Transition Readiness Checklist

Per [ProductiveShop](https://productiveshop.com/founder-led-sales/):

> "Step away from founder-led sales when your process is fully documented, revenue targets are consistently met, the founder's time is stretched thin, and there's enough data to train new reps."

| Readiness Criterion | Status | Action Required |
|--------------------|--------|-----------------|
| Documented sales process | Partial | Complete playbook |
| Repeatable demo flow | Yes | Document variations |
| Qualified pipeline of 3+ months | Building | Increase inbound |
| 10+ closed deals for pattern | In progress | Close more deals |
| Defined ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) | Yes | Validate with data |
| Win/loss analysis | Minimal | Implement CRM tracking |

## 3. Phase 1: Direct Sales Team Build (Months 1-6)

### 3.1 Organizational Evolution

Per [GTMnow Sales Structure](https://gtmnow.com/sales-team-organizational-structure/):

> "The assembly line becomes the backbone (SDR → AE → AM/CSM). Organizations also introduce solutions engineering for complex demos and RevOps to stabilize process, tooling, and data quality."

**Phase 1 Team Structure:**

```
                    ┌─────────────┐
                    │   Founder   │
                    │   (CEO)     │
                    └──────┬──────┘
                           │
              ┌────────────┴────────────┐
              │                         │
       ┌──────┴──────┐          ┌───────┴───────┐
       │   Sales     │          │    Product    │
       │   Leader    │          │    (PLG)      │
       └──────┬──────┘          └───────────────┘
              │
    ┌─────────┼─────────┐
    │         │         │
┌───┴───┐ ┌───┴───┐ ┌───┴───┐
│  SDR  │ │  AE   │ │  CSM  │
│ (1-2) │ │ (1-2) │ │  (1)  │
└───────┘ └───────┘ └───────┘
```

**Hiring Sequence:**

| Role | Month | Rationale |
|------|-------|-----------|
| **Head of Sales** | 1-2 | Process owner, builds team |
| **AE (Enterprise)** | 2-3 | Close enterprise deals founder sources |
| **SDR** | 3-4 | Build top-of-funnel |
| **CSM** | 4-5 | Retain and expand existing |
| **AE (Mid-Market)** | 5-6 | Handle SMB/mid-market volume |

### 3.2 Role Definitions

Per [Winning by Design](https://winningbydesign.com/resources/blog/saas-role-definitions/):

**SDR (Sales Development Representative)**
- **Quota:** 15-20 qualified meetings/month
- **Compensation:** $60K base + $20K variable
- **Tools:** LinkedIn Sales Navigator, Outreach, ZoomInfo

**AE (Account Executive)**
- **Quota:** $500K-1M ARR/year (varies by segment)
- **Compensation:** $100K base + $100K variable (OTE $200K)
- **Skills:** Technical acumen, financial services experience preferred

**CSM (Customer Success Manager)**
- **Metrics:** Net Revenue Retention >110%, NPS >50
- **Compensation:** $80K base + $20K variable
- **Focus:** Onboarding, adoption, expansion

### 3.3 Sales Process Documentation

**Required Playbooks:**

| Playbook | Owner | Deadline |
|----------|-------|----------|
| Qualification Framework (BANT/MEDDIC) | Head of Sales | Week 4 |
| Discovery Call Script | AE | Week 6 |
| Demo Flow by Segment | AE + Product | Week 8 |
| Objection Handling | Head of Sales | Week 10 |
| Proposal Template | Head of Sales | Week 12 |
| Negotiation Playbook | Founder + Head of Sales | Week 14 |

**CRM Requirements:**
- Salesforce or HubSpot implementation
- Stage-based pipeline with exit criteria
- Activity tracking and forecasting
- Win/loss analysis workflow

## 5. Phase 3: Partner Channels (Months 6-18)

### 5.1 Partnership Pyramid

Per [PartnerStack](https://partnerstack.com/articles/saas-channel-readiness-scaling-partnerships-pyramid):

> "The best approach for most SaaS companies is to first start with affiliate partners, scale up into referral partners, and finally launch their reseller partner program."

```
                    ┌───────────────┐
                    │   RESELLER    │  Year 2+
                    │   PARTNERS    │  (15-25% revenue)
                    └───────┬───────┘
                            │
               ┌────────────┴────────────┐
               │      REFERRAL           │  Month 6-12
               │      PARTNERS           │  (10-15% revenue)
               └────────────┬────────────┘
                            │
          ┌─────────────────┴─────────────────┐
          │         AFFILIATE / TECH          │  Month 3-6
          │            PARTNERS               │  (5-10% revenue)
          └───────────────────────────────────┘
```

### 5.2 Partner Type Details

#### Tier 1: Affiliate & Technology Partners (Months 3-6)

**Affiliate Partners:**
- Quant finance bloggers and influencers
- Trading education platforms
- YouTube financial content creators

**Commission Structure:**
- 20% of Year 1 revenue
- One-time payment on conversion
- 90-day cookie window

**Technology Partners:**
- Cloud providers (AWS, GCP, Azure marketplace)
- Data providers (Polygon, Alpaca)
- Broker integrations (Interactive Brokers)

**Model:**
- Joint marketing
- Co-branded content
- Integration listings

#### Tier 2: Referral Partners (Months 6-12)

**Target Partners:**
- Quantitative finance consultants
- Trading system integrators
- Compliance/legal advisors to funds
- Prime broker coverage teams

**Referral Program Terms:**
| Tier | Partner Type | Commission | Payment Terms |
|------|--------------|------------|---------------|
| Bronze | Individuals | 10% Y1 | On invoice payment |
| Silver | Boutique firms | 15% Y1 | Monthly |
| Gold | Strategic partners | 20% Y1 + 5% Y2 | Monthly |

**Qualification:**
- 1+ qualified referral to join
- 3+ closed deals for Silver
- 10+ closed deals for Gold

#### Tier 3: Reseller Partners (Year 2+)

Per [Bessemer GTM Guide](https://www.bvp.com/atlas/the-gtm-guide-to-building-saas-channel-partnerships):

> "Launching on sophisticated reseller channels requires dedicating significant time and resources, with typical timelines sitting closer to 12 to 18 months."

**Target Reseller Categories:**

| Category | Examples | Value Add |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| **Prime Brokers** | Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, BNP | Capital intro, credibility |
| **Data Vendors** | Bloomberg, Refinitiv, FactSet | Distribution reach |
| **Cloud Marketplaces** | AWS, Azure, GCP | Procurement ease |
| **Regional Partners** | Asia, EMEA specialists | Geographic expansion |

**Reseller Economics:**

| Element | Terms |
|---------|-------|
| Discount to list | 25-40% |
| Minimum commitment | $500K pipeline/year |
| Enablement investment | $50-100K per partner |
| Co-marketing fund | 5% of partner revenue |
| Margin structure | In perpetuity (SaaS model) |

## 6. Multi-Touch Sales Motion

### 6.1 Outbound Sequencing

Per [Cognism](https://www.cognism.com/blog/the-pod-b2b-sales-team-of-the-future):

> "One touchpoint isn't enough. Mix LinkedIn, email, and phone into a coherent sequence."

**Standard Outbound Sequence:**

| Day | Channel | Action |
|-----|---------|--------|
| 1 | LinkedIn | Connection request with personalized note |
| 3 | LinkedIn | Message with value proposition |
| 5 | Email | Intro email with relevant content |
| 7 | Phone | Call attempt (leave voicemail) |
| 10 | Email | Follow-up with case study |
| 14 | LinkedIn | Engage with their content |
| 17 | Email | Different angle (pain point specific) |
| 21 | Phone | Final call attempt |
| 25 | Email | Break-up email |

**Segment Variations:**

| Segment | Primary Channel | Sequence Length | Personalization |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Indie | Email + Community | 14 days | Low (scaled) |
| Mid-Market | LinkedIn + Email | 21 days | Medium |
| Enterprise | Multi-threaded | 45 days | High (custom) |

### 6.2 Enterprise Account-Based Motion

**Named Account Strategy:**

| Element | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| **Account Selection** | 50-100 named accounts based on ICP fit |
| **Research** | Deep dive on each account (10+ data points) |
| **Multi-Threading** | 3-5 contacts per account |
| **Personalization** | Custom content per account |
| **Touches** | 15-20 touches over 90 days |
| **Executive Involvement** | Founder involvement for tier-1 accounts |

**Buying Committee Mapping:**

| Role | Priority | Messaging Focus |
|------|----------|-----------------|
| CTO / Head of Quant | Primary | Technical capabilities, integration |
| CFO / COO | Secondary | ROI, cost savings |
| Head of Risk | Influencer | Risk management features |
| Traders | Users | UX, performance |
| Compliance | Gatekeeper | Regulatory alignment |

## 10. Risk Mitigation

### 10.1 Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Per [Arise GTM](https://arisegtm.com/blog/go-to-market-challenges-for-scale-up-and-enterprise-b2b-saas-companies):

> "Many scale-ups prematurely scale sales before fully validating their product-market fit or repeatable sales model. This often leads to overextended teams 'tripping over themselves' with inconsistent sales execution."

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| **Scaling too fast** | Validate repeatability before each hire |
| **Founder bottleneck** | Delegate early, document obsessively |
| **Wrong first hires** | Hire sales leader first, let them build |
| **Partner distraction** | Focus on direct until $1M+ ARR |
| **Channel conflict** | Clear rules of engagement from day 1 |
| **CAC payback > 12mo** | Per [Martal](https://martal.ca/smb-vs-enterprise-lb/): "Keep CAC payback under 12 months" |

### 10.2 Decision Gates

| Gate | Criteria | Action if Not Met |
|------|----------|-------------------|
| Gate 1 (M3) | 3+ repeatable closed deals | Delay hiring |
| Gate 2 (M6) | AE at 50% quota | Coach or replace |
| Gate 3 (M9) | CAC payback < 18mo | Adjust pricing or motion |
| Gate 4 (M12) | Net Revenue Retention > 100% | Invest in CSM |
| Gate 5 (Y2) | Partner revenue > 10% | Double down on partners |
